A BIT ABOUT SRI LANKA


AN ORIGINAL HOLIDAY EXPERIENCE

"Sri Lanka is an island nation located in the Indian Ocean, just off the southeastern coast of India.The island is rich in natural resources, and has a diverse economy based on agriculture, mining, fishing, manufacturing, and tourism. Shaped like a teardrop, the island of Sri Lanka measures about 255 miles (415 km.) from north to south, and about 135 miles (220 km.) from east to west, with a total land area of about,300 square miles."



Tourism

Sri Lanka is known as a popular tourist destination. Sri Lanka comprises many tourist destinations such as beaches, rainforests, wildlife, rich cultural heritage, and many other architectural buildings that have been recognized worldwide. One of the main attractions in Sri Lanka are the wild parks like Yala, Kumana, and wilpattu. Tourists love to watch wild animals and birds in their natural habitats. Another famous attraction is the beaches in Sri Lanka. Tourists enjoy swimming, snorkeling, and diving. They also enjoy the seafood cuisines offered which are fresh and packed with flavour.

Spices

Eastern cuisines have been immensely influenced by herbs and spices. Spices are an element used by cooks to highlight and enhance the flavor of the dish. Herbs and spices are not only used in food but is also used for various medical purposes. Sri Lanka is no stranger to the spice world. From ancient times, Sri Lankan cinnamon has been famous all around the world. Merchants from everywhere have come to Sri Lanka during ancient times to obtain cinnamon as it is known to be the most flavorsome compared to other countries

"Some examples of Sri Lankan Spices:

  1. Ceylon Pepper
  2. Ceylon Cloves.
  3. Ceylon Cardamom.
  4. Nutmeg and Mace.
  5. Ginger.
  6. Vanilla."


Cultural Diversity

Sri Lanka has a great cultural diversification. Sri Lanka has people with different religions. Each reliogion has its own colorful rituals and practices which add to the culture of the country. Other events like colonial impact, wealth, globalization have also influenced the current culture and traditions in Sri Lanka.
