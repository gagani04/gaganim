YALA NATIONAL PARK

--------------------------------

Yala National Park is situated in south east region of Sri lanka. The entrance to the part is 12km from kirinda and is known as Palatupana. There are other parks like Kumana and wilpattu, but Yala is the best place to witness a diversity of wildlife.

LEOPARD

Sri Lankan leopards (Panthera Pardus Kotiya) are said to be a distinct sub-species from their Indian neighbors. Leopards can be seen throughout the park, though best period for enjoying the sights of leopards is during January to July.

ELEPHANT

The Sri Lankan elephant is one of three subspecies of the Asian elephant. The species as a whole is recognized as being the largest land animal in Asia and the Sri Lankan type is the largest of the species.
