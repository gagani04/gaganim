Dambulla Cave Temple

The Five Caves

The temple is made of 5 caves that were converted into shrine rooms. The most impressive cave temples are the Anuradhapura temple and the Polonnaruwa temple which were built in the 1st century B C E to 993 CE and 1073 to 1250 respectively. The Dambulla Rock allows visitors the ability to the divotors to access the temple. A beautiful scenary can be witnessed from the Dambulla rock and it is very famous among tourists. King walagamba, nissankamalla, and other hindu deities were also represented in these caves. Lord Buddha’s most devoted disciple Ananda is also portrayed in the caves. The largest cave has an area of 52m from east to west and 23m from front to back.

Statues

150 statues of Buddhist order and the country’s history are portrayed in the shrine rooms. Many of the epochs of Sinhala sculptures and art are represented by these paintings and statues. The largest statue of Lort Buddha is 15 meters long and the others vary in size. There is one cave with over 1500 paintings of Lord Buddha.
